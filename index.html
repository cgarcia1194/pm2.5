Brooklyn residents along the BQE suffer from higher exposure to PM2.5, the most harmful air pollutant

Where the BQE goes, so do higher levels of exposure to the pollutant PM2.5

By CARLOS GARCIA

December 14, 2023

In Brooklyn, levels of PM2.5, the most harmful air pollutant, are higher in neighborhoods along the Brooklyn-Queens-Expressway.

NYC.gov released data for annual average measurements of PM2.5 for the entire city in 2022. The data reveals that residents in Brooklyn who are closer to the BQE suffer from higher exposure to the pollutant.

PM2.5, or fine particulate matter, is a term for tiny particles that pollute the air. They are caused by everyday urban activities like traffic density, truck density, and diesel exhaust in industrial areas.

According to the NYC Department of Health, PM2.5 from traffic causes an estimated 320 premature deaths per year.

“The people who live directly under the BQE are predominantly Black and Brown and predominantly lower-income,” says Rose Uscianowski, an organizer at Transportation Alternatives. “They have been harmed for decades because of this structure that the city and state created.”
